In our first attempt of coding an acutal website, we were given the task to code our own portfolio. Here i used the first edition of my portfolio design as a template, and goal to achieve.


The requirements for the assigment was as below stated:


  • Upload an online version of you portfolio to your webhotel
  • Create a repository for your portfolio project
  • A fitting project structure (filename, folders)
  • A flawless code (validated)

When it came to coding my "dream-portfolio" i quickly realised that my previous design, was at that point out, of my coding skills. Therefore i had to cut the design down to my own limitations in order to complete the assignment. I would still keep the first design as a template, and with it its layout.


First problem i ran into was the background. I couldnt make the background fit the screen, and after spending too much time of the same problem, i had to move forward. I ended up with an alternative solution, which was to create a solid background color (grey) and crop out an image that i could place at the bottom center. With this solution the same layout was still kept, but with a slightly more boring and dull look.


Next i was trying to place the title on the left side on the front figure, which i ended up just centering above it instead.


Also on the frontpage i was needed to get rid of the blurry glass panel at the bottom right. Simply due to the fast i was not able figure out how to create the glass-effect.


Beside the issues mentioned above, the frontpage stayed more or less the same, with a navebar representing the original pretty good.


For the rest of the pages on the site, the layout and overall look was kept the same as the original, but only lacking the window/glass panel effect again.

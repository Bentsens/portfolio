This is for our first assignment, which was an introduction to photshop. In this assignment we had to use photoshop to manipulate a photo taken on our fieldtrip. Worth mentioning that i worked a fair amount in photoshop before this assigment was given.


The requirements for the assigment was as below stated:


  • Remove an element: some on the picture has to be removed, and the whole you create in which to do so has to be recovered so it match the original image

  • Add an element: Something that was not on the original image has to be added, so it looks like it was there from the beginning

  • Correct light or add an atmosphere byusing filters or color correction

  • Crop the image and maybe adjust the image rotation

  • Format and size: The image has to be in format 4:3 and saves so the siez is â‰¦ 1 M pixel

  • Upload the original, unedited image side by side with you own edited version


I have chosen to use the first listed image of an old wrecked car, because i had the idea of restoring it. In the process of doing so i ended up with using all of my skills in photoshop.


In quick summery, i masked out the car so i could reduce the height of it. I removed all of the stickers by eyedropping nearby colors of the car, and then repainted the cars body. Then i applied a layer of texture to the body by sampling a piece of oxidated metal and laying it on top of the car parts with an overlay. With a destroyed front bumper, i took a front bumper from another old car and resized it to fit with transfom and perspective. I also added new headlights and wheels with the same method. The windows is simply just painted in there with different colors and opacity applied to the brush tool. I wanted to keep the "wrecked-look" so i did not refurbish the car door, instead i drew som metal pipes. Then some of the last touches was simply to change the background, add some smoke behind the car, and apply a camera raw filter.
